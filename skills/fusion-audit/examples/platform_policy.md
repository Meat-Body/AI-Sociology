# Example: Platform Policy

## Scenario

A digital platform changes its recommendation and monetization policy to increase revenue and reduce moderation cost.

## Audit Summary

- Surplus created: platform revenue, moderation efficiency, ranking control, attention allocation.
- Insufficiency addressed: unclear unless the platform names user safety, creator sustainability, or public information quality.
- Capture risk: high if revenue and ranking power remain centralized.
- Externality bearers: creators, small businesses, users with low visibility, moderators, downstream communities.
- Buffer status: weak if there is no appeal, explanation, transition period, data export, or fork path.

## Example Score

- Surplus creation: 4

- Return flow: 1

- Buffer protocol: 1

Total: 13

Classification: `Extractive`

## Recommended Revisions

- Publish machine-readable policy explanations.
- Provide appeal and audit channels for affected creators and communities.
- Add a transition window before monetization changes take effect.
- Provide data export and portability.
- Share ranking impact metrics where safe.
- Create local adaptation rules for different communities.
- Separate public-safety moderation from revenue optimization.
- Establish external review for policies that affect livelihoods or public discourse.
